Elderly Online Safety Guide

Top Tips for Elderly Online Safety:

  • Keep your personal information private. Avoid sharing sensitive information like your full name, address, or financial details online.
  • Be cautious of unsolicited emails or messages. Do not click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for your online accounts and consider using a password manager to keep track of them.
  • Keep your devices and software up to date to protect against security vulnerabilities.
  • Learn how to recognize common online scams and fraud schemes.
  • Consider using privacy settings on social media platforms to control who can see your information.
